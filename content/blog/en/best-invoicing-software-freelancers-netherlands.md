## What Makes Dutch Invoicing Different?

If you have used invoicing software in the UK, US, or elsewhere, Dutch invoicing has specific requirements you need to meet:

### Required on every Dutch invoice:

- **KvK number** (8-digit Chamber of Commerce number)
- **BTW identification number** (format: NL123456789B01)
- **Correct VAT rate** (0%, 9%, or 21% depending on service/product)
- **Separate VAT breakdown** (subtotal excl. VAT, VAT amount, total incl. VAT)
- **Sequential invoice numbering** with no gaps
- **Client's VAT number** (for EU business clients using reverse charge)

### Tax reporting requirements:

- **Quarterly VAT returns** filed via Mijn Belastingdienst
- **Annual income tax return** (aangifte inkomstenbelasting)
- **ICP declaration** if you invoice EU business clients

Most international tools cannot handle these requirements. You need software built specifically for the Dutch market.

## Why International Tools Don't Work in the Netherlands

Many freelancers try to use international invoicing software they already know — QuickBooks, FreshBooks, Wave, or Zoho Invoice. These tools have fundamental problems for Dutch freelancing:

### ❌ Missing Dutch VAT rates

International tools only support single VAT rates or limited customization. The Netherlands requires three rates (0%, 9%, 21%) with specific rules about which applies when.

### ❌ No KvK/BTW number fields

Your KvK number and BTW identification number are legally required on every invoice. International tools lack dedicated fields for these, forcing you to add them manually (creating risk of errors).

### ❌ No quarterly VAT reporting

The Belastingdienst requires quarterly VAT returns in a specific format. International tools cannot generate the required breakdown of output VAT, input VAT, and ICP declarations.

### ❌ Non-compliant invoice numbering

Dutch law requires sequential invoice numbers with no gaps or duplicates. Some international tools allow you to manually set invoice numbers, creating compliance risk.

### ❌ No iDEAL payment integration

iDEAL is the standard payment method in the Netherlands (used in 70%+ of online transactions). International tools do not integrate with Dutch payment providers like Mollie.

## Do You Need Full Accounting Software?

**Short answer:** Not when you start. Invoicing software is enough for most new freelancers.

**Invoicing software** handles:

- Creating and sending invoices
- Tracking which invoices are paid/unpaid
- Generating VAT overviews for tax returns
- Basic expense tracking

**Full accounting software** adds:

- Bank account synchronization
- Automatic transaction categorization
- Balance sheet and profit/loss reports
- Integration with accountant systems

**When to upgrade:**

- You have 20+ transactions per month
- You hire an accountant who needs specific software
- You want automated bookkeeping
- You need detailed financial reports

Most freelancers start with invoicing-only software like Declair and add accounting later if needed.

## How to Switch Invoicing Software

Worried about getting locked into the wrong platform? Switching is easier than you think:

**Before switching:**

1. Export all invoices as PDF (required for 7-year retention)
1. Export client data as CSV
1. Note your last invoice number

**When setting up new software:**

1. Import your client list
1. Set your next invoice number to continue the sequence
1. Upload past invoices for reference (optional)

**What you must keep:**

- All invoices (PDF) for 7 years
- All receipts and expenses for 7 years
- VAT return confirmations

Store these separately from your invoicing software (cloud backup, external drive, or both).
